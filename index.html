Why Now

A once-in-a-generation compliance shift

The NAR settlement, state regulatory fragmentation, and emerging AI governance rules have created the perfect storm.

August 2024

NAR Settlement Takes Effect

Mandatory written buyer broker agreements before every showing. Elimination of MLS compensation fields. New commission disclosure rules. The biggest compliance shift in decades.

2024 – 2025

State Regulatory Fragmentation Accelerates

50 states, 50 different rule sets — all diverging post-settlement. Different CE hours, forms, disclosure requirements, advertising rules, and trust account regulations. Multi-state compliance becomes exponentially harder.

2025 – 2026

New Compliance Categories Emerge

Colorado AI Act (bias audits for housing AI). 15–20 states with data privacy laws. Expanded climate/environmental disclosure. Cybersecurity requirements for brokerages.

2026+

The Managing Broker's Burden Peaks

"Failure to supervise" becomes the #1 charge against managing brokers. E&O insurers begin requiring documented compliance programs. License renewal tied to demonstrated compliance.
